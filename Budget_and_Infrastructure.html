Case Study 1: Sorting Project Quotations by Cost

In the city of Aryavarta, the government plans multiple infrastructure projects and receives quotations from contractors: [₹12,00,000, ₹10,00,000, ₹15,00,000, ₹11,00,000, ₹13,00,000]. The challenge is to identify the most cost-effective contractor efficiently.

Algorithms Used:

Merge Sort:

How It Works: The list is divided into smaller parts, sorted individually, and merged back in order.

Example: Split: [₹12,00,000, ₹10,00,000] and [₹15,00,000, ₹11,00,000, ₹13,00,000]. Merge: [₹10,00,000, ₹11,00,000, ₹12,00,000, ₹13,00,000, ₹15,00,000].

Result: Contractor B (₹10,00,000) is the most cost-effective.

mergesort

Quick Sort:

How It Works: A "pivot" value is chosen (₹12,00,000), and values are rearranged into those less than the pivot and those greater. The process is repeated until the list is sorted.

Example: Sorted list: [₹10,00,000, ₹11,00,000, ₹12,00,000, ₹13,00,000, ₹15,00,000].

Benefits: Sorting ensures a structured and informed decision-making process, saving costs and time.

quicksort

Case Study 2: Sorting Buildings by Time to Completion

In the city of Aryavarta, five building projects are planned with construction times: [90, 120, 80, 150, 110] days. The goal is to prioritize faster projects to meet deadlines.

Algorithms Used:

Heap Sort:

How It Works: A min-heap is built where the smallest time is placed at the top. Repeatedly extracting the smallest time results in a sorted list.

Building Time (Days)
Building C 80
Building A 90
Building E 110
Building B 120
Building D 150

Result: Building C (80 days) is prioritized first.

heapsort

Bubble Sort:

How It Works: Adjacent values are compared and swapped if needed. After several passes, the list becomes sorted.

Building Time (Days)
Building C 80
Building A 90
Building E 110
Building B 120
Building D 150

Benefits: Timely completion of projects avoids penalties and improves public satisfaction.

bubblesort

Case Study 3: Identifying the Shortest Path for Utility Installation

In Aryavarta, the city plans to install pipelines between six areas with the following distances (in meters):

From To Distance (m)
Central Square Tech Park 500
Central Square City Mall 800
Tech Park Education Hub 700
City Mall Industrial Zone 1,000
Education Hub Residential Area 600
Industrial Zone Residential Area 900

Algorithms Used:

Dijkstra’s Algorithm:

How It Works: Start from Central Square and calculate the shortest distance to each area. Repeat until all areas are visited.

Example: Central Square → Tech Park → Education Hub → Residential Area (total: 1,800 meters).

dijkstra

Bellman-Ford Algorithm:

How It Works: Relax all edges repeatedly to find the shortest path, considering any delays. The result is the same shortest path as above.

Benefits: Efficient utility installation reduces costs and improves service delivery.

bellmanford

Case Study 4: Water Pipeline Installation with Minimum Cost

In Aryavarta, the city needs to connect six areas with the following pipeline costs (in INR):

From To Cost (₹)
Central Square Tech Park ₹50,000
Central Square City Mall ₹80,000
Tech Park Education Hub ₹70,000
City Mall Industrial Zone ₹1,00,000
Education Hub Residential Area ₹60,000
Industrial Zone Residential Area ₹90,000

Algorithms Used:

Kruskal’s Algorithm:

How It Works: Sort edges by cost and pick the smallest ones without forming loops.

Result: Central Square → Tech Park → Education Hub → Residential Area → Industrial Zone → City Mall (total: ₹3,50,000).

kruskal

Prim’s Algorithm:

How It Works: Start at any area and connect it to the nearest unvisited area. The result matches Kruskal’s algorithm.

Benefits: Minimizing costs allows for better resource allocation.

prim

Case Study 5: Scheduling Workers for Tasks

In Aryavarta, the city assigns tasks to workers based on skill levels and task urgencies:

Worker Skill Level Task Urgency (1-5)
A 3 5
B 1 3
C 4 2
D 2 1
E 5 4

Algorithms Used:

Heap Sort:

How It Works: Create a max-heap based on urgency and skill. Extract the highest priority worker-task pair repeatedly.

Result: Task 5 → Worker A, Task 4 → Worker E.

heapsort
