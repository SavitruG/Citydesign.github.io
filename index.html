What this course is about?

The "Design and Analysis of Algorithms" course delves into the foundational principles of computational problem-solving, emphasizing both theoretical and practical aspects.

It explores structural data management through trees and graphs, enabling efficient organization and traversal of data.

The course also covers essential sorting and searching algorithms, ensuring optimal data manipulation and retrieval.

By connecting each concept to real-world applications, it illustrates the relevance of algorithms in fields such as network optimization, database management, and artificial intelligence.

This hands-on approach equips learners with the skills to design efficient algorithms and critically analyze their performance, fostering innovative problem-solving capabilities.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Algorithm analysis and complexity theory
  • Divide and conquer algorithms
  • Greedy algorithms
  • Dynamic programming
  • Graph algorithms
  • Backtracking and branch-and-bound

Data Structures and Algorithms Studied

Math Basics

- We started by understanding fundamental math concepts, critical for this course. - This included calculating time and space efficiency for solutions and recurrence relations. - Examples included solving problems like Tower of Brahma and the n-queens problem.

Math Foundations

- We then discussed math foundations, including orders of growth. - Using orders of growth, we analyzed the efficiency of solutions in more detail.

Structured Data Management

- We explored different data structures for efficient organization and retrieval. - We started with n-ary trees, focusing on binary trees.

From binary trees, we learned searching algorithms leading to binary search trees.

We explored various tree structures like 2-3 trees, AVL trees, and Red-Black trees, each with its strengths in terms of efficiency for insertion and deletion.

Other structures covered include Tries (efficient for searching based on prefixes), Skip Lists (fast insertion), and Fenwick Trees (efficient point updates and range queries).

Heaps were introduced for efficient priority queuing, along with their associated algorithms.

Finally, we learned about Breadth-First Search (BFS) and Depth-First Search (DFS), fundamental algorithms for graph traversal.
