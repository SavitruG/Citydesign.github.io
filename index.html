What this course is about?

The "Design and Analysis of Algorithms" course delves into the foundational principles of computational problem-solving, emphasizing both theoretical and practical aspects. It explores structural data management through trees and graphs, enabling efficient organization and traversal of data. The course also covers essential sorting and searching algorithms, ensuring optimal data manipulation and retrieval. By connecting each concept to real-world applications, it illustrates the relevance of algorithms in fields such as network optimization, database management, and artificial intelligence. This hands-on approach equips learners with the skills to design efficient algorithms and critically analyze their performance, fostering innovative problem-solving capabilities.

Key topics covered in this course include:

  • Algorithm analysis and complexity theory
  • Divide and conquer algorithms
  • Greedy algorithms
  • Dynamic programming
  • Graph algorithms
  • Backtracking and branch-and-bound
